## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not immediately AI-looking. Barlow Condensed + red-on-navy automotive palette and the list-style services section are deliberate brand-appropriate moves. Breakdown points are structural: 9 sections all use the same h2 + p.mt-3 heading treatment, the same section-py spacing, and no variation in compositional weight. The uniformity reads as template-generated even if individual sections look intentional in isolation. White/surface-1 sections carry almost no committed color — the brand register has permission for "committed" strategy but execution lands closer to "restrained."

**Deterministic scan:** 2 findings.
1. `single-font` (BaseLayout.astro:56) — Partially false positive. Both Barlow and Barlow Condensed are declared. But they share identical letterform DNA; only contrast axis is width, not character.
2. `layout-transition` (global.css:216) — Real finding. `transition: width` on `.nav-link-slide::after` causes layout thrash. Should use `transform: scaleX()`.

## Overall Impression

The bones are solid: Barlow Condensed display type, navy/red palette, semantic HTML, strong mobile sticky CTA, well-structured accessibility. What's missing is rhythm and compositional commitment. Every section opens the same way, closes the same way, and breathes the same way. The gallery is entirely placeholder SVGs when real workshop photos are the highest-trust element the page could show.

## What's Working

1. **Mobile sticky CTA bar.** WhatsApp + phone call, full-width, bottom of viewport, correct safe-area insets, slide-in animation. One tap to contact from anywhere on the page.
2. **Services list layout.** Using a bordered list rather than a card grid is the right call — scans faster, avoids the identical-card-grid antipattern.
3. **Semantic HTML and form accessibility.** `<article>` for reviews, `<details>`/`<summary>` for FAQ, aria-live on error messages, aria-describedby wiring, skip-to-content link. The accessibility groundwork is genuinely good.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Gallery shows 8 placeholder SVGs — no real imagery shipped**
Why: The gallery is the highest-trust element for a local mechanic. Visitors verifying the workshop is real get colored rectangles. GallerySection.astro includes detailed alt text describing real photos that were never added. Brand register is explicit: "Zero images on a brief that implies imagery is a bug."
Fix: Add real workshop photos to public/images/gallery/. Even 3-4 real photos beats 8 SVG placeholders.
Suggested command: /impeccable harden

**[P1] Booking form buried at bottom — hero CTA scrolls past 7 sections**
Why: Product success metric is "booking within 45 seconds." Hero CTA links to #rezerwacja in ContactSection, the very last content section. Mobile user scrolls past About, Process, Services, Gallery, Reviews, FAQ, and Locations.
Fix: Move BookingSection.astro (already exists) early in the page order — after ProcessSection or ServicesSection. Or add a mid-page booking anchor.
Suggested command: /impeccable shape booking-form-placement

**[P2] All section headings use identical treatment — no hierarchy**
Why: Every h2 uses section-heading: clamp(2.8rem, 5.5vw, 4.5rem) 700 weight. No section announces itself as more important than others.
Fix: Reserve full section-heading size for 2-3 highest-priority sections. Reduce others by ~30%.
Suggested command: /impeccable typeset

**[P2] Section spacing is metronomic — same section-py everywhere**
Why: clamp(3rem, 8vw, 6rem) applied identically to all 9 sections. Eliminates pacing and rhythm on a long scroll.
Fix: Vary spacing by section density and dark/light transition direction.
Suggested command: /impeccable layout

**[P2] Width-property transition on nav underline — layout thrash**
Why: transition: width triggers layout recalculation every frame. On mid-range devices this causes jitter.
Fix: Use transform: scaleX(0→1) with transform-origin: left center instead.
Suggested command: /impeccable optimize
