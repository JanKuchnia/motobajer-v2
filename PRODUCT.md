# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Car owners in Myślenice and surrounding area (southern Poland, near Kraków) who need a mechanic. Most arrive via a mobile Google search after noticing a warning light, needing seasonal tyre swap, or looking for a service recommendation. They are practical, trust-oriented, and local: they want to confirm the shop is real, nearby, professional, and easy to contact in under 30 seconds. A secondary user is a returning customer booking a specific service.

## Product Purpose

MOTOBAJER is a local car repair workshop and parts shop that has operated in Myślenice since 2008 under Stanisław Bajer. The site exists to:
1. Establish immediate trust (reviews, longevity, EuroWarsztat network membership)
2. Show exactly what services are offered
3. Let customers book a visit or call with one tap
4. Give both physical addresses with live maps

Success looks like: a mobile visitor calls or submits a booking request within 45 seconds of landing.

## Brand Personality

Established, no-nonsense, local. A workshop that has been fixing cars since before smartphones: reliable, unhurried, competent. Not a startup, not a chain — a family business with a real person's name on the sign. Voice: direct Polish, specific, unpretentious.

## Anti-references

- Generic car franchise landing pages (Midas, AutoTeile24 equivalents): over-produced, national brand energy, stock photos of smiling uniformed mechanics
- SaaS-style single-page apps with animated dashboards and "streamline your experience" copy
- Glassmorphism automotive enthusiast sites
- Any site where the design tries harder than the business

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the conversion.** Every element that doesn't build trust is competing with elements that do. Pare down.
2. **Local over generic.** Real addresses, real phone numbers, real Google reviews, real map pins. No stock-photo smiles.
3. **One tap to contact.** The primary CTA on every screen-size is a phone call or booking. Never buried.
4. **Specificity over abstraction.** Concrete service names, specific years, actual staff names. Not "our team" but "Stanisław Bajer".
5. **Honest mechanical.** Design language should feel like a well-maintained workshop: precise, functional, clean without being sterile.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Older user base is likely (car owners 35+), so generous touch targets, readable body text, and clear visual hierarchy are essential. Smooth scroll and entrance animations must respect prefers-reduced-motion.
